# FeeHunt Website Strategy

## Purpose

The FeeHunt website at `feehunt.pro` should clearly explain the product, build trust, support beta distribution, and prepare users for the public commercial launch.

The site should communicate four core promises:

- Privacy-first Gmail analysis
- Local processing on the user's computer
- Honest pricing with no hidden fees
- Cancel anytime with a clear self-service path

## Site Structure

### Home (`/`)

Primary landing page for new visitors.

Goals:

- Explain what FeeHunt does.
- Show the main benefits: find subscriptions, save money, clean the inbox, and prepare for future phishing protection.
- Present trust signals: 14-day free trial, cancel anytime, privacy-first, works locally.
- Guide users toward starting a free trial or downloading the beta.

Primary CTA:

- Start Free Trial

Secondary CTA:

- Download Beta

### Pricing (`/pricing`)

Commercial plan comparison.

Goals:

- Show Basic and Family plans.
- Explain trial terms.
- Reinforce cancel-anytime policy.
- Prepare for future checkout integration.

Plans:

- Basic: €5.90/month, 1 Gmail account
- Family: €9.90/month, up to 3 Gmail accounts

### Download (`/download`)

Windows download and setup page.

Goals:

- Provide the latest Windows beta download.
- Explain installation steps.
- Explain Google OAuth setup in plain language.
- Include beta expectations and safety notes.

### FAQ (`/faq`)

Trust and objection-handling page.

Goals:

- Explain privacy, local processing, Gmail support, subscriptions, trial, billing, and account limits.
- Reduce confusion before checkout or download.

### Privacy Policy (`/privacy`)

Formal privacy policy.

Goals:

- Explain local processing.
- Explain OAuth-based Gmail access.
- Explain what data is stored locally.
- State that FeeHunt does not sell user data.

### Terms of Service (`/terms`)

Formal terms page.

Goals:

- Define product use.
- Explain beta limitations.
- Explain subscription and cancellation terms.
- Clarify acceptable use and disclaimers.

### Feedback (`/feedback`)

Beta feedback collection page.

Goals:

- Let beta users report bugs, confusion, product ideas, false positives, and cancellation success stories.
- Prepare for lightweight customer support workflows.

### Contact (`/contact`)

General contact page.

Goals:

- Provide a clear way to contact FeeHunt.
- Direct users to support, feedback, and business inquiries.

## Launch Priorities

1. Publish Home, Download, Pricing, FAQ, Privacy, Terms, Feedback, and Contact pages.
2. Keep pricing visible even before checkout is live.
3. Use a clear "Coming Soon" state for checkout and plan upgrades until Stripe is integrated.
4. Keep privacy and local processing visible on every commercial page.
5. Add customer portal and cancellation links before enforcing paid plans.
